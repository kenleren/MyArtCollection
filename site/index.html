Why it feels different

Made for collectors, not back offices.

The app direction is intimate and practical: beautiful enough to enjoy, structured enough to trust, restrained enough to avoid false certainty.

See the collection, not a table.

Artwork records start with the object and its story, then add documents, location, and confidence notes around it.

Know what is missing.

Keep it grounded.

Trust boundaries

Serious records without overclaiming.

A collector record should help you prepare, compare, and remember. It should not pretend to be an authenticator, appraiser, or insurer.

01

Private by design

Launch copy stays local-first and careful. Any future cloud or AI service must be described only when its behavior is actually shipped.

02

User-confirmed facts

Suggestions and research can support a draft, but final collection details stay under your review.

03

Evidence stays attached

Receipts, certificates, appraisals, and provenance notes live beside the artwork record so context does not disappear.

04

Made for decisions

Reports are shaped for conversations with insurers, family, movers, and estate planners, not for marketplace hype.

05

Collector scale

Designed for home collections with enough art to need structure, without the cost or complexity of gallery registrar systems.

06

Beautiful enough to revisit

The best record is the one you actually maintain. Archivale should feel like opening your own private archive.
