Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Archivale handles data for Archivale: Art Records, the local-first artwork record app and the public Archivale website.

Developer and app identity: Archivale / Archivale: Art Records. Last updated: July 9, 2026. Privacy questions and deletion or export requests should be sent through the support page.

01

Local-first records

Artwork records, photos, notes, locations, values, and supporting documents stay on your device by default unless you choose an export, support, beta, diagnostic, or future-gated online feature.

02

User-controlled capture

Camera, photo, file, and document access are used for actions you start, such as taking a photo, importing an image, attaching a PDF, or importing and exporting CSV files.

03

No sale of data

Archivale does not sell personal or sensitive user data. The app is not an advertising, marketplace, appraisal, or social-network product.

04

No mobile provider keys

The mobile app must not contain OpenAI or other provider keys. The live OpenAI broker is fail-closed and not live unless a later reviewed server-side rollout separately enables it.

Sharing And Processors

Archivale does not sell personal or sensitive user data. Archivale shares or makes data available only in limited situations:

  • With service providers that operate the website, beta distribution, diagnostic, support, hosting, or future server-side AI systems used for Archivale.
  • With Google Play, Firebase, or related Google services when you use a release channel or build where those services are enabled.
  • With OpenAI or another reviewed AI provider only if a later server-side broker rollout is enabled and you explicitly start an eligible online research request.
  • With destinations you choose, such as a CSV export, report export, file share sheet, support message, or other user-directed transfer.
  • When required to comply with law, security, fraud prevention, abuse handling, or a business transfer with appropriate notice.

Local artwork records, photos, documents, notes, and collection contents are not intentionally sent through Firebase telemetry. The Firebase Crashlytics facade is limited to fixed, sanitized diagnostic categories when it is enabled for an internal beta release.

Security Handling

  • Local records and attachments are stored in app-private storage by default. App-private storage is not the same as encryption, and this policy does not claim local encrypted storage unless a later reviewed release implements it.
  • Files are not placed into a public gallery or shared folder by default. Exports remain app-private until you choose where to save or share them.
  • Network features that are enabled for beta, support, diagnostics, or future AI broker use HTTPS or provider-managed secure transport where applicable.
  • The mobile app must not contain OpenAI, Firebase admin, signing, or other provider secrets. Provider calls, if ever enabled, must go through reviewed server-side systems.
  • Crash and operational telemetry must avoid artwork titles, artist names, locations, prices, notes, filenames, paths, prompts, responses, source URLs, tester lists, tokens, and secrets.

Retention, Deletion, And Export

Area Retention and control
Local app data Artwork records, photos, documents, reports, and exports remain on your device until you edit records, delete records, delete app data, uninstall the app, or save/share an exported copy elsewhere. Deleting the app or local records does not delete copies you already exported or shared outside Archivale.
CSV import and export CSV files you import are used to create or update local records. CSV exports are created only when you start an export and are controlled by the destination you select. You may use export paths to retrieve your own record data where the shipped release supports them.
Accounts The current app and public website do not expose user-created Archivale accounts. Because there is no in-app account creation in the current release posture, account deletion is not an active app flow. Local deletion, export, beta removal, and support request paths are still available as described here.
Beta signup queue When beta collection is enabled in a reviewed deploy, pending beta-interest records are retained for up to 90 days unless Archivale is actively contacting the person. Rejected records are retained for up to 30 days for audit and abuse review. Deletion requests are routed through support and targeted for manual handling within 30 days.
Support inquiries Support messages are kept as needed to respond, maintain support history, handle safety or abuse issues, and satisfy reasonable operational or legal needs. You can request deletion through the support page, subject to records that must be retained for security, abuse, or legal reasons.
Diagnostics and beta distribution Crash and diagnostic records, when enabled for a beta release, are kept for beta stability review according to the configured Firebase project settings and operational need. Tester participation or beta removal requests should be sent through support.
AI broker data, if later enabled The current live broker is not enabled by default. If a later reviewed server-side AI broker is enabled, uploaded image derivatives must be deleted from broker-controlled temporary storage after completion or terminal failure, idempotency tuple state may be retained for up to 24 hours, content-free operational telemetry may be retained for up to 30 days, and OpenAI Responses requests must use store=false. This does not by itself prove Zero Data Retention or remove the need for provider review.

Permissions And Local Behavior

Archivale asks for device access only when the app feature needs it. Camera access supports taking artwork photos. Photo and file access supports importing images, documents, and CSV files. Export and share flows use the destination you choose. The app should not use these permissions for background tracking, advertising, or unrelated collection.

Data Safety Consistency

Google Play Data safety answers must be reviewed against the exact Android build, release track, permissions, SDK inventory, Firebase flags, beta posture, export behavior, and any enabled AI broker path. This policy is intended to stay consistent with those answers, but it is not a Play Console submission, a deployment approval, or legal advice.
