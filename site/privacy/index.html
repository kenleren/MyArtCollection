Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

Archivale is built for private collectors, families, and collection managers who want records they can trust, revisit, and preserve. This policy explains how the website and app handle the information you choose to keep with Archivale.

Last updated: July 9, 2026. Privacy questions, deletion requests, and export requests should be sent through the support page.

01

Private by default

Artwork records, photos, notes, locations, values, and supporting documents stay on your device unless you choose to send them for support, export, diagnostics, or Archivale research help.

02

User-controlled capture

Camera, photo, file, and document access are used for actions you start, such as taking a photo, importing an image, attaching a PDF, or importing and exporting CSV files.

03

No sale of data

Archivale does not sell personal or sensitive user data. The app is not an advertising, marketplace, appraisal, or social-network product.

04

Archivale research help

When you ask for research help, Archivale uses the smallest useful request and keeps the rest of your record private. You stay in control of what gets sent and what gets saved.

When Information Leaves Your Device

Archivale does not sell personal or sensitive user data. Archivale shares or makes data available only in limited situations:

  • With service operators that keep the website, support route, diagnostics, hosting, or Archivale research help running.
  • With destinations you choose, such as a CSV export, report export, file share sheet, support message, or other user-directed transfer.
  • When required to comply with law, security, fraud prevention, abuse handling, or a business transfer with appropriate notice.

Local artwork records, photos, documents, notes, and collection contents are not intentionally sent through diagnostics. Any reported crash or operational detail is limited to fixed, sanitized categories.

How Archivale Protects Records

  • Local records and attachments are stored in app-private storage by default. App-private storage is not the same as encryption, and this policy does not claim local encrypted storage unless a later reviewed release implements it.
  • Files are not placed into a public gallery or shared folder by default. Exports remain app-private until you choose where to save or share them.
  • Network features for support, diagnostics, and research help use HTTPS or service-managed secure transport where applicable.
  • The mobile app must not contain service secrets. Research help requests are routed through protected server systems.
  • Crash and operational telemetry must avoid artwork titles, artist names, locations, prices, notes, filenames, paths, prompts, responses, source URLs, contact lists, tokens, and secrets.

Retention, Deletion, And Export

Area Retention and control
Local app data Artwork records, photos, documents, reports, and exports remain on your device until you edit records, delete records, delete app data, uninstall the app, or save/share an exported copy elsewhere. Deleting the app or local records does not delete copies you already exported or shared outside Archivale.
CSV import and export CSV files you import are used to create or update local records. CSV exports are created only when you start an export and are controlled by the destination you select. You may use export paths to retrieve your own record data where the shipped release supports them.
Accounts The current app and public website do not expose user-created Archivale accounts. Because there is no in-app account creation, account deletion is not an active app flow. Local deletion, export, early access removal, and support request paths are still available as described here.
Early access interest Pending early access interest records are retained for up to 90 days unless Archivale is actively contacting the person. Rejected records are retained for up to 30 days for audit and abuse review. Deletion requests are routed through support and targeted for manual handling within 30 days.
Support inquiries Support messages are kept as needed to respond, maintain support history, handle safety or abuse issues, and satisfy reasonable operational or legal needs. You can request deletion through the support page, subject to records that must be retained for security, abuse, or legal reasons.
Diagnostics Crash and diagnostic records are kept for stability review according to operational need. Early access removal requests should be sent through support.
Archivale research help When you ask for research help, uploaded image derivatives are deleted from temporary research storage after completion or terminal failure, duplicate-request state may be retained for up to 24 hours, and content-free operational details may be retained for up to 30 days. Archivale is configured to minimize retention in the research service where those controls are available.

Permissions And Local Behavior

Archivale asks for device access only when the app feature needs it. Camera access supports taking artwork photos. Photo and file access supports importing images, documents, and CSV files. Export and share flows use the destination you choose. The app should not use these permissions for background tracking, advertising, or unrelated collection.
